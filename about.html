Our Story

Victoria and Chrystal first met in the copy room of a public charter high school in Houston, TX. Victoria was a math teacher, while Chrystal taught English and Literature. Both graduates of The University of Texas at Austin, the two quickly discovered that they shared more than just an alma mater. They also shared a passion for serving diverse communities through education. Since then, Victoria and Chrystal have dedicated themselves to providing schools, organizations, families, and students with the support and resources necessary to expand their access to higher education.

Our Beliefs

Our Vision

It is our vision that groups that have historically had inequitable access to education will feel just as prepared and positioned for post-high school success as other students. Our hope is that our collaboration with local communities fosters a culture of excellence in education for all.

Our Mission

Dean+Golden education bridges the gap in access to institutions of higher education by providing high-quality standardized test preparation, academic tutoring, college advising, and financial planning to students and their families.

More About Me

Victoria Dean

CEO|Co-Founder

"As Chief Executive Officer, I have the pleasure of fostering relationships with larger organizations and smaller families alike. Additionally, I enjoy leading the Math and Science tutoring efforts.

In high school, I was known equally as a 'nerd' and an athlete. I was the student that went to regionals for the 4x200m in track and competed in a UIL AP Computer Science competition in the same year. My love for Math and Science was apparent very early on, so much so that I’ve tutored students in these subjects since ninth grade.

Naturally, being from Texas and having a love for Math, I went to The University of Texas at Austin and studied Petroleum Engineering. During undergrad, mentoring through Student Engineers Educating Kids (SEEK) stimulated my interest in education as a career opportunity. After working in oil and gas for two years, I decided to revisit my passion for education. I left my engineering career to spend time in the classroom. I taught ninth through eleventh grade Algebra I/II in my hometown of Houston, TX before beginning my Ph.D. program at the Georgia Institute of Technology in the School of Civil and Environmental Engineering. Simultaneously, I am pursuing my MBA at the Georgia Tech Scheller College of Business.

Since matriculating at Georgia Tech, I’ve been involved with K-12 outreach, worked as a content expert for STEM curriculum training for teachers in Gwinnett County, and served as a newly appointed Associate Regional Director of the Southeast region of the Global Learning and Observation to Benefit the Environment (GLOBE) program."

Chrystal Golden

COO|Co-Founder

"As the Chief Operating Officer, I oversee the programming elements of the company, I conduct research on trends in education, and I serve as the lead for our college advising services.

I was a first-generation college student whose parents had no idea how to navigate the post-secondary admissions process. That said, getting to and through college posed its own set of difficulties. After transferring twice and attending three different universities in the span of four years, I graduated from The University of Texas at Austin. My teachers, counselors, and professors were integral to my success as a student. Knowing how much of an impact these educators had on me, I decided to become one through Teach for America in Houston. I spent three years teaching English II, AP English Literature, Health, Speech, and a College Readiness course.

Helping my students start to see themselves as ‘college-bound’ was the highlight of my career and an educator and I knew that I wanted to continue doing similar work.

While completing my graduate studies at Emory University, I spent two years working in a college advising and tutoring capacity which confirmed my love for educational consulting."

Terrance Gresham

Board Member

"My role as a Board Member is to assist with the financial planning curriculum and help lead efforts to develop strategic partnerships.

As a high school senior, my heart was set on attending one university. I got accepted, attended Summer Orientation, packed my things, and drove nearly 400 miles – only to learn that I didn’t have enough financial aid to cover the first semester. This humbling experience became my catalyst for wanting to know more about the college admissions and financial aid process. After earning a bachelor’s degree in Business Administration from Buffalo State and spending a year in corporate America, I began my career in higher education after realizing my passion for being an advocate in college admissions. Along the way, I’ve led diversity recruitment initiatives for several universities including Emerson College, Northeastern University, and the Georgia Institute of Technology.

I've advised hundreds of students, earned a master’s degree in Higher Education from Northeastern University, and worked tirelessly for underrepresented students seeking access to premier colleges. Keeping one foot in the business world, I completed an MBA from Georgia Southern University to bridge my personal interests in business and education."
